Inspiring Women in Boxing

Meet the legends who changed the game and inspired generations.

Barbara Buttrick

Known as “The Mighty Atom,” she was a pioneer of women’s boxing in the 1950s. She fought when women weren’t allowed to and opened the ring for future fighters.

Lucia Rijker

One of the most feared boxers of the 1990s, undefeated and powerful, Rijker was known as “The Most Dangerous Woman in the World.”

Laila Ali

Daughter of Muhammad Ali, she carved her own legacy with an undefeated record and became a role model for strength and elegance in the ring.

Katie Taylor

Olympic gold medalist and undisputed lightweight champion, Taylor helped push women’s boxing to global recognition in the 2010s.

Amanda Serrano

One of the most decorated female boxers of all time, Serrano holds titles in seven weight divisions and is a proud Puerto Rican icon.
